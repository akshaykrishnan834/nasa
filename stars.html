Fascinating Facts About Stars

Sirius

Sirius is the brightest star in the night sky, located in the constellation Canis Major.

Distance: 8.6 light-years

Type: A1V main-sequence star

Betelgeuse

Betelgeuse is a red supergiant star in the constellation Orion, known for its distinctive reddish color.

Distance: 642.5 light-years

Type: M1-M2 supergiant

Polaris

Polaris, also known as the North Star, is located nearly directly above the North Pole.

Distance: 433 light-years

Type: F7:Ib supergiant

Alpha Centauri

Alpha Centauri is the closest star system to the Solar System, located about 4.37 light-years away.

Distance: 4.37 light-years

Type: G2V main-sequence star

Procyon

Procyon is the brightest star in the constellation Canis Minor and one of the closest stars to Earth.

Distance: 11.46 light-years

Type: F5 IV-V

Altair

Altair is the brightest star in the constellation Aquila and is known for its rapid rotation.

Distance: 16.7 light-years

Type: A7 V main-sequence star
