The Planets of Our Solar System

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It's a rocky planet with extreme temperature variations between day and night.

Venus

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in our solar system.

Earth

Earth is our home planet and the only one in the solar system known to support life. It has water, a breathable atmosphere, and a diverse range of ecosystems.

Mars

Mars, the "Red Planet," is known for its reddish appearance due to iron oxide on its surface. Scientists believe it may have had liquid water in the past.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant known for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm that's been raging for centuries.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning rings, made of ice and rock particles. It's the second-largest planet in the solar system and another gas giant.

Uranus

Uranus is unique because it rotates on its side. It's an ice giant, with a cold, gaseous atmosphere, and faint rings around it.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun. It's an ice giant like Uranus, and has incredibly strong winds, the fastest in the solar system.
